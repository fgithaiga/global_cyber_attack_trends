# WHO ATTACKS WHO IN CYBERSPACE? A GLOBAL ATTRIBUTION STUDY


# BUSINESS UNDERSTANDING
In today’s highly interconnected digital environment, cyber attacks pose a growing risk to governments, businesses, and critical infrastructure worldwide. Organizations across all sectors rely heavily on digital systems to store sensitive data, deliver services, and conduct operations. As a result, cyber incidents can lead to financial losses, operational disruption, reputational damage, and national security threats.

Decision-makers such as government agencies, cybersecurity firms, and corporate security teams require a clear understanding of global cyber attack trends to allocate resources effectively and strengthen defensive strategies. However, cyber incident data is often fragmented, making it difficult to identify consistent patterns related to attack types, targeted regions, and responsible actors.


## **PROJECT OVERVIEW**

Cyber attacks have become one of the most significant threats to national security, economic stability, and organizational operations worldwide. As digital technologies continue to expand across governments, businesses, and critical infrastructure, cyber threats have increased in both frequency and complexity. Understanding global cyber attack patterns is essential for developing effective cybersecurity strategies and policy responses.

This project analyzes a global cyber incident dataset containing reported cyber attacks across multiple countries, regions, and sectors. Using data-driven techniques, the study examines the types of cyber attacks, their temporal trends, geographical distribution, targeted sectors, and attribution to known threat actors. The analysis aims to identify key patterns and trends that characterize the evolving global cyber threat landscape.

By uncovering insights into who is targeted, how attacks are carried out, and how often perpetrators are identified, this project contributes to a better understanding of global cyber attack behavior. The findings can support cybersecurity planning, risk assessment, and informed decision-making by policymakers, organizations, and security professionals.


##  **PROBLEM STATEMENT**

Despite the increasing number of reported cyber attacks worldwide, there is limited consolidated analysis that clearly explains global trends in cyber attack types, target regions, and attribution patterns. Many cyber incidents are reported in isolation, making it difficult to identify broader patterns over time and across different countries and sectors. Additionally, challenges in attributing cyber attacks to specific actors further complicate global cybersecurity efforts.

Without a data-driven understanding of these trends, governments and organizations struggle to prioritize cybersecurity resources, anticipate emerging threats, and develop effective defense strategies. This project addresses this gap by systematically analyzing a global cyber attack dataset to identify common attack types, temporal trends, targeted countries and sectors, and the extent to which attacks are attributed to known actors. The study seeks to provide empirical insights that can enhance situational awareness and support more informed cybersecurity decision-making.


# OBJECTIVES
The primary business objective of this project is to transform global cyber incident data into actionable insights that support strategic cybersecurity decision-making. Specifically, the project aims to:

Identify the most common cyber attack types.

Understand growth or decline in cyber attacks over time.

Determine the most frequently targeted countries and sectors.

Compare cyber attack patterns across regions and industries.

Evaluate the extent to which cyber attacks are attributed to known threat actors.



# DATA UNDERSTANDING

The dataset includes attribution-related variables that provide information about the suspected actors behind cyber attacks:

attribution_ID – A unique identifier for the attribution record of an incident.

number_of_attributions – Indicates how many times an incident has been attributed to one or more known actors.

These fields are critical for analyzing attribution patterns and understanding the confidence level in identifying the responsible parties. They help highlight which attacks can be reliably traced to specific actors and which remain uncertain.

The dataset also contains source and reporting variables that describe how cyber incidents were detected, recorded, and included in the database:

source_incident_detection_disclosure – Information on how the incident was discovered or disclosed.

inclusion_criteria – Criteria used to determine whether the incident should be included in the dataset.

added_to_DB – Date when the incident was added to the database.

updated_at – Date when the incident record was last updated.

These fields are essential for assessing data reliability, completeness, and potential reporting bias. They provide context on how incidents were captured and the quality of the dataset, which is important for any subsequent analysis or reporting of global cyber attack trends.
